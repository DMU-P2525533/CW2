Flowers

This is a Horse Mint plant. It origins from Africa, Europe and Asia and is part of the mint species. The plant is also used within cooking and teas.

This is a Chinese Astilbe. Originally from China and Japan, it was introduced to western countries in the 20th century. The plant thrives in the shade and comes in a variety of bright colours.

The Agapanthus Preacox only bloom in the summer. Although they are sometimes called 'African Lily', they aren't actaully lilies. They are from the Amaryllis family.

This is an Elder Shrub. As part of the elderberries species, the berries grown are eaten by birds. Elder is toxic to humans except from the flowers and ripend berries.

The Rodgersia herb is native to northen China. It is part of the Saxifragaceae family and blooms in the summer. It is also typically used in ponds.

The Oxeye daisy has many names such as 'Dog daisy' and 'Moon daisy'. It is considered as a common weed with low toxicity levels. It can also reproduce asexually.

This is a Great globe-thistle that has become naturalized in North America. Its scientific name is Echinops Sphaerocephalus and it belongs to the Asteraceae family.

This speicies of Chain fern is called the Rooting chainfern. The average height of this plant is 1.8 meters and is also evergreen. It is most notable for its intresting shape.

As part of the Giant hyssops species, the Anise hyssops has a mint fragrance which attracts bees and birds. It can tolerate drought and can pollinate itself.

Wildlife

This breed of cow is called the Aberdeen Angus. It is one of the most common breeds of cow in the UK. An average female weighs 550kg and males usually weigh a whopping 850kg!

This is a Rosemary beetle (Chrysolina americana). It was first spotted in London in 1994 and can be found betweeb May and October

This breed of swan is called a mute swan (Cygnus olor). It is commonly found in Leicester which is where this photo was taken. They have an average life span of 10 years!

This photo is of a common laybird that was resting on a blue pole. This image was my first time ever using a macro lens!

This is a Greylag goose (Anser anser). It is a common breed in the UK and the rest of Europe.

This is a Ploughshare Tortoise. Sadly, this breed is endangered due to poaching. It is easily identified by its tall shell.
